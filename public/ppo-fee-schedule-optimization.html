PPO Reimbursement and Rising Costs

Across healthcare practices, rising operational costs — including staff wages, equipment, rent, and technology — have increased significantly in recent years. However, insurance reimbursement rates often remain stagnant. For optometry practices, this creates a growing gap between the cost of providing care and the revenue collected from insurance plans.

Optimizing PPO participation and negotiating stronger reimbursement rates can help practices close this gap and maintain healthy financial performance.

Know Your PPO Participation Types

There are three common PPO participation structures:

  • Direct Contract – A direct agreement between the practice and the insurance carrier.
  • PPO Leasing – Insurance carriers share provider networks, allowing additional plans to access the same contracted rates.
  • Umbrella Networks – Multiple insurance plans operate under a larger umbrella network that extends participation automatically.

Understanding which networks your practice is enrolled in is essential, because many practices unknowingly participate in multiple networks with different reimbursement levels.

Increase PPO Reimbursement with Direct Negotiations

Most insurance companies periodically review and adjust their reimbursement rates. However, many practices never request renegotiation and remain locked into outdated fee schedules that no longer reflect the true cost of care.

Correct Coding and Documentation

Accurate CPT and ICD coding is essential to ensure maximum reimbursement from insurance carriers. Coding errors or outdated codes can lead to claim denials, reduced reimbursements, or compliance risks.

Regular coding audits and updated documentation practices ensure that claims accurately reflect the services provided and comply with payer requirements.

Review Claims Before Submission

One of the most effective ways to increase reimbursement is ensuring claims are clean before submission. Incorrect patient information, insurance details, or coding errors can result in preventable denials.

  • Verify patient demographic information
  • Confirm insurance eligibility
  • Validate CPT and ICD coding accuracy
  • Ensure correct payer submission

Clean claim submission significantly reduces denials and accelerates reimbursement timelines.

PPO Reimbursement and Rising Costs

Across healthcare practices, rising operational costs — including staff wages, equipment, rent, and technology — have increased significantly in recent years. However, insurance reimbursement rates often remain stagnant. For optometry practices, this creates a growing gap between the cost of providing care and the revenue collected from insurance plans.

Optimizing PPO participation and negotiating stronger reimbursement rates can help practices close this gap and maintain healthy financial performance.

Know Your PPO Participation Types

  • Direct Contract – A direct agreement between the practice and the insurance carrier.
  • PPO Leasing – Insurance carriers share provider networks allowing additional plans access to the same contracted rates.
  • Umbrella Networks – Multiple insurance plans operate under a larger umbrella network extending participation automatically.

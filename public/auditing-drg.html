Auditing & DRG Review

We perform advanced clinical, coding, and reimbursement audits to identify underpayments, over-denials, and DRG mismatches that directly impact provider revenue.

Our audit methodology is evidence-based, payer-specific, and aligned with CMS, AHA, and commercial payer guidelines.

Why Auditing & DRG Review Matters

Incorrect DRG Assignment

Even small documentation gaps can shift DRG weight downward, resulting in thousands of dollars lost per admission.

Coding vs Documentation Gap

Coders can only code what is documented. We identify missing physician language that weakens claim strength.

Payer Underpayments

Commercial payers frequently underpay DRG and CPT-based claims. Our audits identify contract variance and recovery potential.

Our Audit Process

1. Clinical Documentation Review

Physician notes, discharge summaries, operative reports, and progress notes are reviewed for severity, specificity, and medical necessity.

2. ICD-10 & CPT Validation

Diagnosis and procedure codes are validated against official ICD-10-CM, ICD-10-PCS, CPT, and CMS guidelines.

3. DRG & Weight Analysis

DRG assignment is re-evaluated using MCC/CC logic to ensure correct reimbursement classification.

4. Appeal & Correction Strategy

We prepare appeal-ready audit reports with supporting evidence for payer submission.

Evidence-Based Methodology

We correlate lab values, imaging, vitals, and treatment intensity to justify higher-severity DRG levels.

Benchmarking

Claims are benchmarked against historical payer reimbursements and regional averages.
